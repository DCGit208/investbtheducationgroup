## 2. Pod Economics (Illustrative)

This table answers a client question:

&gt; “If I engage a full 1:50 industrial pod from BTH, what does it cost and what does it actually deliver?”

### 2.1. Columns Explained

**Pod Type**  
The category of industrial work the pod specializes in. On the web page we show:

- **Welding &amp; Fabrication** – structural steel, tanks, pipelines, shutdown weld teams.  
- **Automation &amp; Robotics** – PLC/SCADA, robot cells, upgrades, commissioning.  
- **Maintenance &amp; Reliability** – predictive maintenance, shutdown support, reliability projects.

These are global, high-value service lines in heavy industry.

---

**Velocity (pts/mo)**  
“Velocity” is a capacity metric borrowed from agile and adapted to industrial services:

- It represents how much **completed, accepted work** the pod delivers per month.  
- Higher velocity = more executed scope, more lines covered, more plants served.

Example ranges:

- Welding &amp; Fabrication: **80–120 pts/mo**  
- Automation &amp; Robotics: **90–130 pts/mo**  
- Maintenance &amp; Reliability: **90–140 pts/mo**

This allows you to talk about **output**, not just “number of people”.

---

**Monthly Rate (USD)**  
This is the **billing rate** a client pays per month for an entire 1:50 pod.

On the page:

- Welding &amp; Fabrication: **$60k–$100k/month**  
- Automation &amp; Robotics: **$70k–$115k/month**  
- Maintenance &amp; Reliability: **$65k–$105k/month**

The range captures differences in:

- Geography and cost of labor  
- Risk profile (refinery vs light manufacturing)  
- Scope intensity (code welding vs light fabrication)  
- SLA demands (24/7 cover, critical shutdowns, etc.)  

You can transparently say:

&gt; “A fully certified 1:50 pod with code welding, automation and reliability is typically a 60–100k USD/month asset for an industrial client, depending on risk and scope.”

---

**Typical Scope**  
This column answers: *“What does that pod actually do in the real world?”* For example:

- **Welding &amp; Fabrication:** structural jobs, piping and tank repairs, shutdown weld teams.  
- **Automation &amp; Robotics:** PLC/SCADA work, robot cells, commissioning.  
- **Maintenance &amp; Reliability:** predictive programs, shutdown support, reliability projects.

This moves you away from vague training language and into **contract-ready language**.

---

## 3. Leader ROI Ladder (Illustrative)

The **Leader ROI Ladder** answers a different question:

&gt; “If I become a 1:50 Manufacturing &amp; Industrial Team Leader, what is my potential income as my pod grows?”

It has three components:

1. **Active Commission** – earned by building and stabilizing the 1:50 pod.  
2. **Residual (Managed Services)** – income from recurring industrial contracts.  
3. **Cumulative Annualized Income** – the combined, annualized view.

---

### 3.1. Active Commission

This is the **direct commission** linked to team-building and leadership milestones.

On the page:

- **Month 3:** $14,440  
- **Month 6:** $28,880  
- **Month 12:** $44,321  

These are illustrative figures, but they show a **clear growth trajectory**:

- The leader isn’t “waiting forever” to earn; they see staged growth as they build and stabilize their 1:50 pod.  
- The top figure ($44,321) is the **full team-building commission** once the pod is fully formed and engaged in delivery.

You can say to a potential leader:

&gt; “As you build your 1:50 welding–automation–reliability pod and it begins to deliver contracts, your team-building commission grows to approximately 44,321 USD.”

---

### 3.2. Residual (Managed Services)

Once the pod is deployed into **managed services contracts**, the leader earns **residual income**.

On the page:

- **Month 3:** $120k–$240k  
- **Month 6:** $480k–$960k  
- **Month 12:** $1.2M–$2.4M  

These figures represent the **contract value** under management and the potential income overlay for the leader, when:

- The pod is delivering multiple plant contracts.  
- There are long-term SLAs for welding, automation, reliability, and shutdowns.  
- The industrial portfolio of the leader is scaling.

You can articulate:

&gt; “As your pod stabilizes on managed plant support, your residual income is driven by multiple long-term industrial contracts – that’s why by Month 12 we model 1.2M–2.4M USD in managed services under your scope.”

---

### 3.3. Cumulative Annualized Income

The right-hand column summarizes the **annualized earning band**, combining:

- Active commission  
- Residual from managed services  
- Multiple simultaneous contracts

On the page:

- **Month 3:** $300k–$600k  
- **Month 6:** $1.0M–$2.0M  
- **Month 12:** $2.1M+  

This is **not** a guarantee; it’s a *portfolio view*: what a serious 1:50 industrial leader can aim for once they:

- Fully build their pod  
- Are engaged across multiple industrial contracts  
- Sustain performance, safety, and delivery metrics

This aligns the **leadership narrative** with real-world director-level or multi-plant portfolio incomes.
